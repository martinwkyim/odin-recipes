Creme Brulee

Description

At its most basic, creme brulee is a creamy, pudding-like, baked custard with a brittle top of melted sugar that cracks when you gently tap it with a spoon. The custard is made with heavy cream, eggs, sugar, and vanilla. The melted sugar crust will be slightly warm or room temperature when served, but the custard below should be cold.

Part of the joy of eating creme brulee is a study in contrast: You use the edge of your spoon to gently crack the hard sugar surface, in order to expose the creamy custard beneath. Youâ€™ll get a little crunchy topping and custard in each bite.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 10 tablespoons sugar, divided
  • 1 vanilla bean (or 1 teaspoon vanilla extract)
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt

Steps

  1. Make the custard
  2. Bake the custard
  3. Torch the top to caramelize
